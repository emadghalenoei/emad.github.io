About me

I have a passion for understanding the world through data. I was trained as a probabilistic researcher during my Ph.D. in Geomatics Engineering at the University of Calgary. Then, I implemented parallel computations using Massage Passing Interface (MPI) on independent processors, which efficiently collect millions of massive 3-D models from data.

My experiences led me to have the right skills at the right time when machine learning, probabilistic, and data-driven models gained widespread traction earlier last decade. Since then, I have worked on several data-driven models projects, which were helping the development of new model clustering forms.

With both a data science and software programming background, I developed locally adaptive models and data-driven algorithms as well as Python codes to serve the model or algorithm.

I love solving complex and important problems and am currently focused on using probabilistic and machine learning to improve locally model resolution based on data information. This is advancing quickly and will make a tremendous impact on science, and I want to help ensure the model-data relationship is understood properly.
